## The Difference That Matters: One-Time Credits vs Refreshing Quotas

Most creators don’t quit because the model is “not good enough.” They quit because the plan doesn’t match their posting rhythm.

- **One-time credits** are great when you just want to test output quality. They are terrible for building a routine.
- **Weekly/monthly refresh** is what makes a tool usable for consistent publishing—even if the quota is smaller per cycle.

Runway ML’s free credits are the classic one-time model: you can do a couple of serious experiments, then you hit the paywall.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use free AI video generators for commercial content?**

Commercial usage rights vary by platform and model. In practice, paid tiers are usually clearer. If you plan to monetize, treat licensing as a checklist item—not an afterthought.

**Q: Which free AI video generator works best for Instagram Reels?**

For Reels, prioritize **vertical format support** and an export policy you can publish with. A workflow/template approach helps if you’re posting repeatedly; one-time credit plans are better for testing.

**Q: How many videos can I create monthly on free plans?**

It depends on each tool’s quota system. The practical takeaway: **weekly/monthly refresh plans support routines; one-time credits don’t.**

**Q: Do free AI video generators add watermarks?**

Some do (InVideo AI), some block downloads (Synthesia), and some restrict output via limited credits (Runway). Always check export rules before you invest time into a pipeline.

**Q: Can I make vertical videos for TikTok using free tools?**

Yes—just make sure the tool supports 9:16 and the free plan’s export is publishable (watermarks/download restrictions matter more than people admit).
